## Northern Lights Photography Tips

Successfully photographed the aurora on 5 out of 7 nights. Here's what worked:

### Equipment
- **Camera:** Full-frame DSLR (Canon 5D)
- **Lens:** Wide-angle f/2.8 or faster
- **Tripod:** Essential for long exposures
- **Spare batteries:** Cold drains them quickly
- **Intervalometer:** For time-lapses

### Settings
- **Manual mode**
- **ISO:** 1600-3200 depending on brightness
- **Aperture:** f/2.8 or wider
- **Shutter:** 8-15 seconds (depends on aurora movement)
- **Focus:** Manual focus on infinity

### Apps Used
- **Aurora forecast apps** - checked KP index and cloud cover
- **Weather apps** - clear skies are essential
- **Google Maps** - for finding dark spots

## Practical Information

### Driving
- **4x4 rental:** Recommended, especially if going off Route 1
- **Speed limits:** Enforced with cameras, be careful
- **Fuel:** Stations can be far apart, fill up regularly
- **Weather:** Can change quickly, check road conditions daily

### Budget
- **Flights:** $400 (off-season deals)
- **Car rental:** $700 for week (4x4)

- **Accommodation:** $800 (mix of guesthouses and hotels)
- **Food:** $400 (groceries + some restaurants)

- **Good hiking boots** - essential
- **Camera gear** - for aurora and landscapes
- **Snacks** - food can be expensive
