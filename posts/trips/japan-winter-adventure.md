## Overview

Japan in winter is magical. From Tokyo's illuminated streets to Hokkaido's powder snow, this two-week journey showcased the best of Japanese winter. This was my third visit to Japan, but my first in winter, and it completely changed my perspective on the country.

## Tokyo (Days 1-4)

### Arrival & First Impressions

Flying into Narita at night, I took the Narita Express to Tokyo Station. The city was already dressed in winter illuminations, and the crisp December air was refreshing after the flight.

**Hotel:** Park Hyatt Tokyo (Shinjuku)
- The same hotel from "Lost in Translation"
- Incredible views from the 52nd floor
- Outstanding breakfast buffet
- Perfect base for exploring

### Highlights in Tokyo

#### Day 1-2: Traditional & Modern Tokyo
- **Senso-ji Temple** in Asakusa - beautiful in the morning mist
- **Nakamise Shopping Street** - picked up traditional crafts and snacks
- **Tokyo Skytree** - stunning 360¬∞ views of the metropolis
- **Shibuya Crossing** at night - as chaotic and amazing as expected
- **TeamLab Borderless** - mind-blowing digital art museum

#### Day 3: Tsukiji & Food Culture
- **Tsukiji Outer Market** - arrived at 6 AM for the freshest sushi breakfast
- **Sushi Dai** - worth the 2-hour wait, incredible quality
- **Cooking class** in Shinjuku - learned to make proper ramen and gyoza

#### Day 4: Day Trip to Nikko
- **Toshogu Shrine** covered in light snow - absolutely breathtaking
- **Lake Chuzenji** - serene winter landscape
- Returned to Tokyo for farewell dinner at a Michelin-starred kaiseki restaurant

## Kyoto (Days 5-7)

Took the Shinkansen to Kyoto (2.5 hours). The bullet train experience itself is remarkable - smooth, punctual, and comfortable.

**Accommodation:** Traditional Ryokan in Gion District

### Kyoto Highlights

#### Temples & Gardens
- **Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)** - stunning against winter skies
- **Fushimi Inari Shrine** - hiked to the summit early morning to avoid crowds
- **Arashiyama Bamboo Grove** - peaceful and otherworldly
- **Philosopher's Path** - quiet winter walk along the canal

#### Cultural Experiences
- **Traditional tea ceremony** in Gion
- **Geisha spotting** in the evening (respectfully, from a distance)
- **Kaiseki dinner** at the ryokan - 12 courses of seasonal perfection
- **Onsen (hot spring) experience** - learned proper etiquette

#### Day Trip: Nara
- **Todai-ji Temple** - the giant Buddha statue is awe-inspiring
- **Nara Park** - fed deer crackers (they're very pushy!)
- **Kasuga Taisha Shrine** - hundreds of stone lanterns

## Takayama (Days 8-9)

A charming mountain town that felt like stepping back in time.

**Travel:** Limited Express train through stunning mountain scenery

### Takayama Experience
- **Old Town** - preserved Edo-period streets
- **Morning Markets** - local produce and crafts
- **Sake breweries** - tasting fresh sake in the snow
- **Hida beef** for dinner - some of the best beef I've ever had
- **Local festivals** - lucky enough to see winter festival preparations

## Hokkaido (Days 10-14)

The main event: Japan's winter wonderland.

**Base:** Niseko (Days 10-12), Sapporo (Days 13-14)

### Niseko - Skiing Paradise

Niseko is famous worldwide for its powder snow, and it did not disappoint.

#### Skiing
- **Niseko United** - 4 interconnected resorts
- **Powder quality** - the "Japow" legend is real
- **Night skiing** - a unique experience
- **Backcountry** - hired a guide for a half-day tour
- Conditions were perfect with fresh overnight snowfall

**Accommodation:** Boutique hotel in Hirafu Village

#### Food & Apr√®s-Ski
- **Ramen Alley** - perfect after a day on the slopes
- **Local izakayas** - friendly atmosphere with locals and visitors
- **Whisky tasting** - visited a local distillery
- **Onsen** - soaking in outdoor hot springs while snow falls is incredible

### Sapporo - Urban Winter Culture

Took a bus from Niseko to Sapporo (2.5 hours).

#### Sapporo Highlights
- **Sapporo Beer Museum** - history and tasting session
- **Susukino** - the entertainment district, incredibly lively
- **Ramen Yokocho** - tried authentic Sapporo miso ramen
- **Shopping** - Tanukikoji Shopping Arcade
- **Historical Village** - outdoor museum showing Hokkaido's history

#### Food Adventures
- **Soup curry** - Sapporo's unique dish
- **Genghis Khan (Jingisukan)** - grilled lamb, local specialty
- **Seafood markets** - incredibly fresh crab, uni, and more
- **Local craft beer scene** - surprisingly diverse

## Practical Tips

### Transportation
- **JR Pass** - absolutely worth it for this itinerary
- **IC Cards** (Pasmo/Suica) - essential for trains and convenience stores
- **Reserve seats early** - especially for Shinkansen during winter season

### What to Pack
- **Layered clothing** - buildings are heated, outdoors is cold
- **Good walking shoes** - you'll walk a lot
- **Pocket WiFi** - rented at the airport, worked everywhere
- **Cash** - many places still don't take cards

### Language
- **Google Translate** app with offline Japanese downloaded
- **Basic phrases** go a long way - learned "arigatou gozaimasu" etc.
- Many young people speak some English in cities

### Budget
This trip was a mix of luxury and budget:
- Flights: Premium Economy (booked with points)
- Accommodation: Mix of hotels and ryokan ($100-300/night)
- Food: Mix of cheap ramen ($8) and nice dinners ($100+)
- **Total: Approximately $5,000** including flights, accommodation, food, transport, and activities

## Final Thoughts

Japan in winter exceeded all expectations. The combination of incredible food, rich culture, stunning landscapes, and world-class skiing made this one of the best trips I've ever taken. The Japanese hospitality is unmatched, and even with the language barrier, I always felt welcome.
